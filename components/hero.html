Curriculum Vitae

I am an incoming PhD student in the Khoury College of Computer Sciences at Northeastern University under Prof. Hongyang Zhang and Prof. Tina Eliassi-Rad. Previously, I earned my undergraduate degree from Yuanpei College and the School of Mathematical Sciences at Peking University. I then completed my master's in Statistics at the University of Chicago. I was fortunate to work with Prof. Hao Ge and Prof. X.Y. Han.

My research focuses on:

  1. Mechanistic Interpretability: Mathematically characterize the geometric underpinnings of pervasive patterns in large language models.
  2. Interpretable Architectures: Design and analyze novel model architectures guided by counterfactual reasoning and non-convex optimization.
  3. Real-World Translation: Deploy these interpretability and architectural advances in domains such as traffic, healthcare, and education.
